**Date:** 2026-07-08 · **The biggest LMU-specific gap (the one place LMU departs from established physics).** This does **not** close it — the wiring stays `[Hypo]` — but it **shrinks it to its irreducible core** by demarcating what is borrowed/owned from what LMU alone asserts. ## The claim
LMU identifies **ω = the prior aeon's CCC conformal factor Ω = the α-attractor inflaton** that drives the new aeon's inflation. Before this note it read as one broad, un-owned `[Hypo]` ("invent Ω and call it the inflaton").

## What is actually OWNED (borrowed — not LMU's to prove)

- **L1 — Ω becomes a *physical dynamical scalar* after the crossover.** In CCC the conformal factor Ω / its associated **"phantom" field** is *non-dynamical during the rescaling* (a constraint field; reciprocal **Ω = −ω⁻¹**; Ω→∞ at the crossover, "squashing" the old aeon's remote future onto the new bang) — but it **acquires dynamics and a mass *after* the crossover**. That is **Penrose's own construction** (there the post-crossover field is the erebon). So *"the conformal factor becomes a physical dynamical scalar in the new aeon"* is **part of CCC, not an LMU invention.** `[Fact-th, CCC-owned]` (Penrose 2010; phantom-field equation + the 2023 CCC-consistency refinement, Tod-framework, which even *constrains* the admissible Ω).
- **L2 — a conformal-origin scalar *naturally* has the α-attractor structure.** The α-attractor inflaton **is** a conformal compensator (superconformal α-attractors, Kallosh–Linde 2013); Weyl-invariant R² makes the conformal mode the **Starobinsky-attractor** inflaton (Tang 2020) — and there the *other* conformal d.o.f. is the dark matter, the **same split LMU uses**. So "a conformal scalar → α=1 plateau" is **owned.** `[Fact-th, borrowed]`.
- **L3 — the reciprocal wiring Ω = −ω⁻¹** supplies the geometric identification of old-aeon-future with new-aeon-bang. `[Fact-th, CCC-owned]` (Penrose; scale-factor duality, arXiv:1604.08032).
- **L4 — the Penrose dark-matter conflict is already removed** (DM made at the hot start by post-reheating super-heavy production; `JOINT3_filtered_dm.md`), so Ω is **free** of the erebon role.
- **L5 — precedent that CCC's Ω → an inflaton exists** (Beckwith 2007, arXiv:0709.0330: the Penrose model gives emergent inflaton behaviour).

## What stays `[Hypo]` — the irreducible core (now sharp, not broad)

> **The post-crossover dynamical conformal scalar's potential is the α=1 *inflating plateau*, not (only) the erebon *mass*.**

CCC's phantom-field equation fixes Ω only *up to initial data* (Tod 2023); Penrose reads the post-crossover scalar as the **erebon** (~Planck-mass dark matter, ~10¹⁹ GeV). LMU reads it as the **inflaton** (α=1 plateau, V¹ᐟ⁴~10¹⁶ GeV — a GUT-scale *potential*, not a Planck-scale *mass*). The two readings are **distinguishable by scale and role** (mass-term DM vs plateau-potential inflaton), the DM-conflict is gone (L4), and a conformal scalar α-attracts (L2) — **but no derivation forces the inflating plateau over the erebon-mass reading.** That single reading-choice is the whole residual `[Hypo]`.

## Net — the gap is shrunk, not closed

- **Before:** "Ω = inflaton" — one broad, un-owned hypothesis.
- **After:** L1–L5 are **owned/borrowed** (Ω *does* become a physical scalar post-crossover; conformal scalars *do* α-attract; the reciprocal wiring and the DM-freeing are in place; a CCC→inflaton precedent exists). The residue is **one sharp choice**: the post-crossover conformal scalar *inflates* (α=1 plateau) rather than being dark matter (mass) — a reading of **what the CCC-provided scalar does**, differing from Penrose's DM reading (conflict removed) but not *forced* over it.
- **Status unchanged in kind:** the wiring is still `[Hypo]`; LMU is still a hybrid assembly. What changed is **localization** — the novelty is now one demarcated line, most of the machinery is borrowed, and "is Ω even a physical field?" is answered *by CCC itself* (yes, post-crossover), not left hanging.
- **What would close it (out of reach here):** a derivation, from the CCC phantom-field equation + a naturalness/selection principle, that the post-crossover conformal scalar's potential is the inflating plateau rather than the erebon mass. That is **CCC's own open problem** ("how to fix Ω uniquely"), field-wide — LMU inherits it, does not create it.
