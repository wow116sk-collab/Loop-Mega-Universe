## The attack

LMU's aeon-to-aeon relighting (B-primary) nucleates a Coleman–De Luccia bubble out of a metastable "Φ_gen" false vacuum at ~10¹⁶ GeV. The swampland de Sitter conjecture (Obied–Ooguri–Spodyneiko–Vafa 2018; refined Ooguri–Palti–Shiu–Vafa) forbids a V>0 metastable minimum. **Attack #2:** the same conjecture that *favours* LMU's thawing dark energy *forbids* the relighting false vacuum — the framework cannot accept it selectively.

1. **DE side is swampland-compatible.** c = 2M_p/A_i = 0.741 ~ O(1) satisfies arm A; reproduces ε=0.27, w₀=−0.82. The conjecture *favours* thawing DE. [Fact within the conjecture]

3. **TCC lifetime arm — DEFUSED by V→0.** A persistent GUT-scale de Sitter would be TCC-capped at ~10⁻³⁷ s vs the ~10¹⁰⁰ yr wait (144 orders). But the survivor waits in the **cold L0 / V→0 Minkowski basin** (Fork Y endgame); Φ_gen is accessed only transiently at the flash, and the current thawing-DE epoch is the textbook ~1.7-e-fold TCC-compliant transient. No persistent dS → nothing for TCC to bound.

4. **Structural arm — REAL but WEAK.** The refined conjecture is *pointwise* on V, so it forbids the mere existence of a V>0 minimum regardless of occupation; transience does not reach it. The bridge/sign-flip is over-determined: swampland wants curvature ≤ −0.74 (tachyonic), CDL/thin-wall/GMW require a genuine minimum (curvature +2λv²>0) — opposite signs, no shared value. BUT its whole force rests on the **strong universal pointwise reading holding in the interior of moduli space**, which is unproven and actively contested (KKLT/LVS defended 2019–2025); the only derivable form (OPSV/Hebecker) is asymptotic-only, and in the interior the operative bound is the (already-defused) TCC.

The tex never claims exemption — L636 concedes "the same wall." My earlier "q-theory is LMU's main escape hatch" **overstated**; the document does not rely on it.

6. **P≠0 suffices (defence).** Even with the CDL minimum forbidden, the swampland-*allowed* tachyonic top gives a Hawking–Moss rate Γ~exp(−7.8×10¹²) — strictly >0. Over an unbounded L0, any Γ>0 → ignition. Swampland forces at most a channel swap CDL→Hawking-Moss, **not** P=0. P=0 (the loop-killer) needs a separate absolute-stability argument the conjecture does not supply.

## The hourglass (B-alt) — the user's resolution

The "flip the hourglass" picture = the framework's own **B-alt** (conformal rescale), the model-selectable alternative to flash-nucleation (tex L125). ## The unification (pushing the injection question)

The Ω is paid by a **plateau inflaton** (item D, ~10¹⁶ GeV) reheating. But the plateau that yields F4 (r=0.009, n_s=0.965) **also violates both swampland arms**: |∇V|/V=0.034 ≪ 0.74 (arm A, 22×), η=−0.016 not ≤ −0.74 (arm B, 47×). Swapping minimum→plateau does not escape.

**Attack #2 therefore generalises:** the swampland dS conjecture is hostile to *any* hot-start mechanism — trapped minimum (CDL) or flat plateau (slow-roll) — because a bang needs a flat/trapped V>0 region and the conjecture forbids exactly those. The only swampland-happy scalar is a steep roller: good for thawing dark energy, useless for a bang.

This is the textbook **swampland-vs-inflation tension** (Kehagias–Riotto, Kinney 2018–19). It hits *all* slow-roll inflation, not LMU specifically. Mainstream stance: the strong dS conjecture is too strong (it would kill inflation, our best-tested early-universe physics).

- B-primary (nucleation): plateau **+ forbidden minimum** → more swampland-exposed than standard inflation.
- **B-alt (the hourglass flip): only the plateau → exposure equals standard slow-roll inflation's, no more.** The flip reduces LMU's swampland debt to the irreducible floor every inflationary model already carries.
