# PART B — English (Rigorous Statement, Computable)

**Reminder of scope (unmissable):** what follows *reframes* the time coordinate and *locates* the open problem. It does not solve the relighting/de Sitter-stability question, which is the field-wide Tolman-entropy and measure problem (§8, §9).

## §1 Local: time, mass, and tidal acceleration are one object

For a static mass $M$ the Schwarzschild proper-time rate is, exactly,
$$\frac{d\tau}{dt} \;=\; \sqrt{-g_{tt}} \;=\; \sqrt{1-\frac{r_s}{r}}\,, \qquad r_s=\frac{2GM}{c^2}\,.$$
Weak field ($r\gg r_s$): $\dfrac{d\tau}{dt}\approx 1+\dfrac{\Phi}{c^2}$, with $\Phi=-\dfrac{GM}{r}<0$. Deeper potential $\Rightarrow$ slower clock. The dependence on "mass" is **only through the potential depth $\Phi$**; this is the precise form of "mass slows time." **[Fact]** (Einstein 1915; verified: Hafele–Keating 1971; Chou et al. 2010; GPS). In GR the metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ simultaneously encodes proper time, tidal acceleration (geodesic deviation), and curvature — they are **not separable** locally. **[Fact-th]**

## §2 Two accelerations, with the sign fixed

The second Friedmann equation is
$$\frac{\ddot a}{a} \;=\; -\frac{4\pi G}{3}\!\left(\rho+\frac{3p}{c^2}\right).$$
- **Matter/dust** ($p=0$): $\ \ddot a/a=-\tfrac{4\pi G}{3}\rho<0$ — **deceleration.** More clustered mass $\Rightarrow$ *more* deceleration.
- **Dark energy** ($p=-\rho c^2$): $\ \rho+3p/c^2=-2\rho$, so $\ \ddot a/a=+\tfrac{8\pi G}{3}\rho>0$ — **acceleration.**

So "acceleration grows with the mass that builds wells" is **sign-correct only for the *local* infall field** $g=GM/r^2$; for the *cosmic* scale factor, added mass **decelerates**. Cosmic acceleration lives in **voids** (low $\rho$), gravitational wells in **overdensities** — opposite signs. **[Fact-eq]** (Friedmann 1922; the arithmetic above).

## §3 The cosmic clock: a thawing scalar that fades with time

The framework's dark-energy field $A$ (a field **distinct** from the substrate; do not conflate) obeys, in FRW,
$$\ddot A + 3H\dot A + \frac{dV}{dA}=0\,, \qquad V(A)=\tfrac12 m^2 A^2\;\Rightarrow\; \ddot A+3H\dot A+m^2A=0\,.$$
While $3H>m$ the Hubble friction freezes $A$ (thawing); as $H$ falls to $\sim m$ the field releases and rolls toward $V_{\min}=0$. The framework's integration uses $m\approx0.80\,H_0$, $A_i\approx2.70\,M_p$ and returns $w_0\approx-0.91$, $w_a\approx-0.15$, an acceleration window $a\in(0.61,\,5.5)$, and a Minkowski endgame ($V\!\to\!0$). A single canonical scalar satisfies $w\ge-1$ at all times (**no phantom crossing**). **[Fact-th]** for $w\ge-1$ (Vikman 2005); **[Fact-eq: framework's KG integration, recheckable]** for the numbers. *(Context: the DESI $w_0$–$w_a$ tension is $2.40\sigma$/$2.69\sigma$ and is plausibly a CPL-parametrization artifact — Dinda–Maartens 2025; de Souza et al. 2025 — not a phantom detection. **[Fact]** for the numbers, **[Hypo]** for the artifact reading.)*

## §4 The arrow: entropy monotone, the rate-cycle not

Gravitational clustering *raises* entropy (smooth = low, clumped = high — Penrose's Weyl-curvature hypothesis). A black hole and its evaporation carry
$$S_{\rm BH}=\frac{4\pi k_B\,G M^2}{\hbar c}\,, \qquad S_{\rm rad}=\tfrac43\,S_{\rm BH}\ \ (\text{Zurek 1982}).$$
Total entropy is **monotone up** along L0's time — this *is* the arrow. **[Fact-th]** (Penrose 1979; Bekenstein 1973; Hawking 1975; Zurek 1982). The island proper-time *rate* runs base $\to$ dilated (deep wells at the mass peak) $\to$ base (recovered on full evaporation): a **closed** loop. Entropy does **not** ride it. A closed rate-loop therefore does **not** reverse a direction — the same structural lesson as Problem A, in clock language. **[Fact-th]**

## §5 The bang: re-zero + re-mint, not a background reset (the central reframe)

The hot start does two things, and neither is "resetting background time":
- it **re-zeros the internal proper-time label** (the new aeon counts $\tau$ from 1), and
- it **re-mints the content** (baryons and dark matter form fresh at reheating).

What it does *not* do is reset a background clock. The document's own statement is about the **readability of a record**, not a background reset: *"the crossing erases its own record … no clock survives it … a next-aeon observer cannot read 'previous aeon' from any internal datum"* (record-erasure, not time-reset). Splitting "internal proper-time label" from "background/conformal time" is standard FRW bookkeeping: re-zeroing a coordinate label changes no invariant. **[Fact-th]** Consequently an aeon is a **segment of a continuous L0 background time** and **inherits L0's arrow** — so the arrow needs no separate reset (it is born pointing the right way). This dissolves the "arrow reset at the bang" worry. **[Fact-th]**

## §6 The two readings of "pulling L0 time"

Any cross-boundary map is a Weyl rescale $\tilde g_{\mu\nu}=\Omega^2 g_{\mu\nu}$, under which
$$\text{proper time, length}\propto\Omega\,;\quad \text{energy, temperature}\propto\Omega^{-1}\,;\quad \text{massless field equations invariant.}$$
Two readings of "the aeon clock is L0's clock":
- **$\Omega=1$ (pure re-label).** Aeon clock $=$ re-zeroed L0 clock; no blueshift; $z_{\rm aeon}$ does not arise. **Clean, and matches the framework's primary (nucleation) joint** — but then the hot energy must come from **nucleation** (fresh $\Phi_{\rm gen}$ false-vacuum energy), not from the rescale. **[Fact-eq]**
- **$\Omega\gg1$ (blueshift).** Aeon clock $=\Omega\times$ L0 clock; this **reopens** $z_{\rm aeon}=\Omega-1$ as an undetermined boundary datum. **[Hypo]** — recommend against; use $\Omega=1$.

## §7 Why the open problem is energy-shaped, not time-shaped

Take the energy ledger across one boundary. The terminal flash energy $E_{\rm flash}$ is vastly smaller than a full hot cosmological patch $E_{\rm hot}$; their ratio is the required injection factor $\Omega=E_{\rm hot}/E_{\rm flash}$ (the framework quotes $\Omega\sim10^{20}$). The decisive physics: **in GR there is no global energy conservation unless spacetime admits a timelike Killing vector**, and an expanding FRW admits none (Wald 1984, §11.2; Noether's theorem needs the symmetry). So the factor $\Omega$ is **permitted by GR but supplied by nothing** — that missing source *is* Problem A. Crucially, **entropy is already balanced** across the boundary ($S_{\rm rad}=\tfrac43S_{\rm BH}$, conformally invariant), so the residual is **purely energetic**. The reframe of §5 removes the *time-coordinate* confound and leaves Problem A standing alone in the **energy/entropy** sector — sharper, not solved. **[Fact-th]** *(Auditor note: the framework's absolute figures $E_{\rm hot}\!\sim\!2\times10^{80}$ J vs. $E_{\rm flash}\!\sim\!3\times10^{60}$ J warrant an independent normalization recheck — the observable-patch mass–energy is $\sim\!3\times10^{71}$ J from $\rho_{\rm crit}V_{\rm obs}$ — so treat the **ratio** $\sim10^{20}$ as the robust content, not the absolute normalization. **[Fact-eq]**)*

## §8 The price: the L0 clock is a postulated foliation, and it costs a Tolman debt

"A continuous L0 background time" is a choice of spacelike foliation $\{\Sigma_t\}$. Between **causally disconnected** patches there is no invariant simultaneity (no global Killing time), so this clock is a **regulator/coordinate, not an observable** — precisely the **measure problem** of eternal inflation, which has a toy no-go: **no measure obeying reasonable axioms exists** (Olum 2012, arXiv:1202.3376). The scale-factor-cutoff class is the named literature realization of exactly this "postulate a global clock" move. **[Fact-th]**

And a global clock carries a debt. Define global $S(t)$ along it; the second law gives $dS/dt>0$, so cycles must grow $\Rightarrow$ past incompleteness — the **Tolman entropy conundrum** (Tolman 1931). The standard partial resolution is to hold the *horizon* entropy constant by expanding cycle-to-cycle so the entropy *density* dilutes while the total rises (Ijjas–Steinhardt 2019, arXiv:1904.08022); LMU's strengthening is that L0 is *infinite*, so the reservoir is genuinely unbounded. The coupling: infinitely many trials relax relighting from "must ignite" to "$P>0$" (Guth–Weinberg 1983) — but $\infty\times0=0$, so if de Sitter is truly **stable** ($P=0$; Boddy–Carroll–Pollack 2014) an infinite clock cannot rescue it, whereas if it is **unstable** ($P>0$; Volovik 2020, arXiv:2007.05988) it can. **This stability question is the sharp form of Problem A and is field-wide open.** **[Fact-th]** / **[Hypo]** on which way it resolves.

## §9 Scope, restated

The picture is **internally consistent** and **sign-correct**, and it **legitimately dissolves** the "arrow reset at the bang" worry while **correctly relocating** the open problem to the energy sector. It is a **reframe that connects the framework to the measure-problem and Tolman literature on their own terms** — which has value — but it is **not** a solution: relighting / de Sitter stability remains the open joint, unchanged. Empirical verdicts are deferred to DESI-Y5 ($\sim$2027) on the dark-energy side and to a solved de Sitter-stability result on the theory side.
