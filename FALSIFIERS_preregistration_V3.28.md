## F1 — Dark energy: canonical thawing quintessence, no phantom crossing

- **Prediction.** LMU's dark-energy field *A* is **canonical quintessence with w(z) ≥ −1 at all z** and **cannot cross −1** (Vikman 2005). A thawing field matches DESI's *w₀* **or** *wₐ*, not both cleanly; DESI's apparent *w < −1* crossing is read as a **CPL-parametrisation artifact** (physical non-phantom thawing fits comparably — Dinda–Maartens 2025; de Souza et al. 2025). `[Fact-th]` / `[Hypo]` (the reading).
- **Fires (falsified) IFF:** DESI-Y5 (~2027) **sustains w(z) < −1 at high z at ≳ 3σ, with systematics resolved**, and the crossing **survives a physical (non-CPL) reanalysis**.
- **Does NOT fire if:** the data are `w ≥ −1` but evolving; a phantom hint is `< 3σ` or systematics-limited; the crossing appears only under CPL.
- **Scope (what F1 kills):** a confirmed non-CPL crossing falsifies the **canonical** *A*-field (the spine's choice); a quintom / k-essence upgrade would survive it, at the cost of a ghost. So F1 kills the *canonical* field, not every dark-energy variant.
- **The 3σ line is fixed now and must not be moved.**
- **Test / timeline:** DESI-Y5, ~2027.
- **Status at registration (2026-07):** DESI DR2 rejects ΛCDM at 2.8–4.2σ with a CPL crossing near z ~ 0.5 — this **sharpens** F1 but does **not** fire it (CPL artifact; defence holds). One item to watch.

## F2 — Black-hole spin: isolated over-massive relics spin *high*, merger-built BCGs spin *low*

- **Prediction.** The model forces **isolated over-massive relics (NGC 1277, Mrk 1216) to spin HIGH** (accretion-frozen, bucking the general high-mass → low-spin trend), while **merger-built cluster BCGs spin LOW** — a *decoupling* of spin from mass. Conditional on **coherent cocoon feeding f_coh ≳ 0.15** (open joint J3, `[Open]`). For the carry-through variant, the inherited spin axis tilts past 45° after the survivor accretes ~0.77 of its mass in the new aeon. `[Fact-th]` / `[Open]` (the f_coh condition).
- **Fires (falsified) IF any of:** (a) a local over-massive **isolated relic is measured to spin LOW**; (b) a **clear major merger is found in such a relic** (breaks the "grew fast, never climbed" reading); (c) **the super-Eddington dense-cocoon wind is shown to shut off the hole's *own* accretion (M–σ self-regulation), not merely the host's star formation** — LID-568 currently shows the *latter* (host SF only), which is what the mechanism needs; the *former* would remove the isolated-relic over-mass route.
- **Sharper cluster-side threshold:** on the merger/cluster (low-spin) side, only a confirmed **near-maximal spin a★ ≳ 0.9** would tension the merger-spin-down expectation (a★ ~ 0.55).
- **Test / timeline:** X-ray reflection spectroscopy of accreting relics (now → 2030s); NewAthena + LISA (late 2030s) for the carry-through axis; BHEX-era spin history.
- **Status at registration (2026-07):** M87* spin is **contested** — Drew et al. 2025 (a★ ≳ 0.8, Doppler beaming, a lower limit) vs Wong et al. 2025 (polarimetry, disfavours high spin) — logged as an **offsetting flag** (`[Open — contested]`), not a falsification. The cluster spin-down expectation (a★ ~ 0.55) vs a★ ≳ 0.8 is a tension (factor 2.6–10.6 → an aligned-merger fraction ≥ 62%).

## F4 — Single-instanton origin signature: a primordial tensor ratio in the α-attractor plateau band

- **Prediction.** The ~10¹⁶ GeV ignition (an α-attractor plateau) leaves a primordial **tensor-to-scalar ratio in the plateau band**: **r ≈ 0.0037** (α = 1, Starobinsky, N = 57) up to **r ≈ 0.009** (α = 2.44); under ACT DR6's higher N (69–81), **r ≈ 0.0018** (α = 1). **All below the current bound r < 0.036–0.038** (BICEP/Keck). The (n_s, r) point sits **on the α-attractor locus**; the ignition B-mode is the **only** channel that reaches us (direct-detection GW is symmetry-suppressed and buried ~5–7 orders below the astrophysical background). `[Fact-th, borrowed, conditional on the wiring]`.
- **Fires (falsified) IF:** LiteBIRD-class sensitivity measures **r inconsistent with the α-attractor plateau band** — e.g. a robust detection of **r well above the plateau ceiling** (a large-r, non-plateau signal), **or** an (n_s, r) locus **incompatible with a single-field plateau**.
- **Does NOT fire if:** r is measured anywhere in the plateau band (0.0018–0.009) consistent with the (n_s, r) attractor line, or a null below current sensitivity.
- **Sharper thresholds (from the document):** a single quadratic *A*-field serving as *both* inflaton and dark energy gives **r = 8/N ≈ 0.14 — already excluded** by BICEP/Keck (r < 0.036), which is *why* a separate plateau generator (Starobinsky / α-attractor) is required. A **reverse** line: a firm **r < 10⁻³** would favour the ekpyrotic (Ijjas–Steinhardt) rival over an LMU-type plateau. **Scope:** the Φ_gen ~10¹⁶ GeV ignition scale is **not an axiom**, so a differently-measured r *shifts the scale* rather than killing the loop — F4 is a **comparative / differential** signature, not a make-or-break falsifier of the framework.
- **Test / timeline:** LiteBIRD-class B-mode polarisation, 2030s.
- **Status at registration (2026-07):** consistent (predicted r-band sits under the bound); untested at the required sensitivity. Caveat: r is **not** an LMU-original number — it is Starobinsky's, inherited conditional on the wiring; a confirmation supports the α-attractor ignition, it does not *uniquely* single out LMU.

## F5 — No CCC-style Hawking points or concentric circles (a clean departure from bare CCC)

- **Prediction.** LMU predicts the **ABSENCE** of Penrose-CCC-style **Hawking points** and **concentric low-variance circles** in the CMB. Two mechanisms give the absence: **horizon exile** (every *other* island's terminal flash sits outside our horizon, so no cross-aeon imprint reaches us) and — decisively under the current reading — the **deterministic-flash reset ignites inflation**, whose α-attractor plateau (57+ e-folds) **erases** any prior-aeon imprint. A clean discriminator from *bare* CCC, which predicts the circles. `[Fact-th, conditional on the wiring + inflation]`.
- **Fires (falsified) IF:** a **confirmed detection** of CCC Hawking points / concentric circles. Per the document's own stakes flag, a confirmed detection does not merely re-open a sub-item — it **breaks horizon exile itself**.
- **Reframed for the changed thinking (2026-07):** the document's older caveat — "absence is *predicted* only on the B-primary *nucleation* joint, merely *permitted* on the B-alt *rescale* joint" — is a **residue of the superseded barrier framing**. The reset is now a **deterministic flash → inflation**, and *inflation erases prior-aeon structure regardless of the old nucleation-vs-rescale fork*, so the absence is predicted under the current reading (firmer, not fork-conditional).
- **Test / timeline:** CMB anomaly-pattern searches (Gurzadyan–Penrose 2010; An–Meissner–Nurowski–Penrose 2020; Meissner–Penrose 2025), ongoing; sharpened by future high-resolution CMB.
- **Status at registration (2026-07):** **contested; consistent with absence.** Detection claims disagree — AMNP 2020 report 99.98% significance; Jow & Scott 2020 find ~87% (~1σ) after ring-size marginalisation. No confirmed detection stands. (A confirmed Gpc-scale cross-aeon structure — the Giant Arc / Big Ring, itself contested — would fall in the same basket.)

## F6 — The endgame is a *tiny positive* de Sitter, not a future AdS crunch

- **Prediction (current reading).** LMU's chosen endgame is a **tiny nonzero de Sitter**: V_min = ρ_Λ = (2.3 meV)⁴, **anchored to the measured Λ**, so dark energy **asymptotes to a positive constant** (H∞ > 0, w → −1) — *not* to zero (the older "Minkowski / A→0" body prose, now **superseded**) and *not* to a negative floor (a future AdS transition → crunch). `[Fact-th, conditional]`; the endgame choice is `[Design]`.
- **Fires (falsified) IF:** DESI DR3 / Euclid / Roman establish a **future AdS transition** (dark-energy density crossing to negative, V_min < 0) — contradicting the positive-de-Sitter asymptote the endgame commits to.
- **Reframed for the changed thinking (2026-07):** the document's older "V_min = 0 (Minkowski) vs V_min < 0 (AdS)" discriminator is **superseded on the V_min = 0 side** — the framework now commits to a *tiny positive* de Sitter (the 3.27 revision entries; the body's V_min = 0 / Minkowski prose is left verbatim but flagged superseded). The live discriminator is therefore **positive-dS (LMU) vs future-AdS (rival)**.
- **NOT the same as** the field-wide "**de Sitter *stability*** (Γ > 0 vs Γ = 0)" question, which LMU shares and does **not** list as an open item. F6 is the observational **sign of V_min** (positive vs negative), not the decay-rate question.
- **Honest status (leans rival):** **no fixed σ threshold is pre-registered** for this line, and current data **lean against** it — Gialamas et al. 2025 report a ~93.8% preference for a future AdS transition. Registered here as a **live tension to watch** (DESI DR3 / Euclid / Roman era), not a fixed-threshold falsifier; shared with other positive-Λ models, not unique to LMU.

## Explicitly NOT falsifier lines (recorded for honesty)

These are in the document but are **not** registrable falsifiers — stated so the registry cannot be read as claiming more than it does:

- **Dark-matter nature / the 5:1 baryon:DM ratio** — **matched, not predicted**. LMU re-mints DM as a thermal relic each aeon; the ratio and relic nature are *inputs*, and direct-detection bounds (LZ/XENONnT) enter only as an exclusion corridor the model must fit inside. **No DM-mass or cross-section prediction** (no super-heavy-vs-WIMP claim).
- **The re-initialisation targets — n_s, A_s, r, Ω_k, f_NL, isocurvature** — are **declared inputs** the generator must hit (Planck 2018 values), *not* predictions. (F4's r is the α-attractor *consequence* of the plateau, a distinct use.)
- **CMB spectral distortions (μ, y)** — LMU predicts *no* distortion; satisfied by tens of orders (FIRAS). A consistency pass, not a discriminator.
- **PTA / NANOGrav stochastic GW background** — *supports* the SMBH-merger assumption; a consistency/cleanliness bound, not a unique falsifier.
- **CEAW (Cosmic Entropy Acoustic Waves)** — a within-aeon L2 **consistency re-check** (channel weights Γ_j `[Open]`), explicitly *not* a unique falsifier or a growth knob.
- **The "too-early / lump-seeded" seed-flux channel** — a bare "carry-through prediction" with **no observable or threshold** in the document; the doc itself cautions that high-z AGN over-massiveness can be a virial-mass artifact (up to 1 dex) and anchors the relic claim on *dynamical* masses. Not registrable as-is.
