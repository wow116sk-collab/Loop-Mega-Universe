*A five-minute, no-equations telling of the Loop Mega Universe (LMU) picture — V3.26.

## Act 1 — We are in the middle of the book

When someone says "the Big Bang was the beginning of everything," this framework raises
a hand and asks: *whose* beginning? In the LMU picture, our universe is one **aeon** —
one chapter in a longer book. Our Big Bang is not the book's first page; it is the first
page of *our chapter*. Which makes the interesting question: how did the previous
chapter end, in such a way that its ending became our first page?

## Act 2 — Dark energy is a mountain, not a plateau

Right now the universe's expansion is accelerating. The standard picture says the thing
pushing it is a constant — the same push forever. In the LMU picture, dark energy is a
**field** (called A) that behaves like a ball resting on a slope. While the universe is
young and expanding fast, the expansion itself acts as friction and pins the ball in
place — so it impersonates a perfect constant. As the universe ages, the friction fades,
and **the ball wakes up and rolls**.

The consequence: cosmic acceleration is not a plateau but a **mountain**. It climbs,
peaks almost exactly in our era (a coincidence the document flags rather than hides),
and then descends — acceleration ends after the universe grows a few more times over.
Eventually the ball reaches the valley floor and oscillates, and a fast-oscillating
field behaves exactly like dust: it thins away with the expansion. In plain words,
**dark energy can die**. This is the framework's first pre-registered test: DESI is
measuring right now whether the equation of state drifts the "thawing" way. DESI-Y5,
around 2027, sharpens or breaks it.

## Act 3 — The black-hole ladder: last one standing

Fast-forward very far. Stars are dead, the gas is spent, the universe is cold, dilute,
and clumpy. What remains flows down a **ladder**: gas and stellar remnants (rung L4)
feed the black holes at galaxy centers (L3); galaxies merge and their holes merge with
them, building the giants at the centers of clusters (L2); and inside each island of
matter, merging has only one possible endpoint — **a single survivor** (L1), a hole of
roughly a hundred billion solar masses that has won every contest and now sits alone.

One detail from the ladder earns its keep: holes that grow by orderly feeding spin
*up*, while holes that grow by chaotic mergers get their spins randomized *down*. That
asymmetry is the framework's second appointment with data — black-hole spin in the
coming BHEX era. (Honest status note: the two current methods for measuring M87*'s spin
presently disagree with each other, and the document records the question as open.)

## Act 4 — The most patient thing that has ever existed

What does the last survivor do next? It **waits** — in a way the word "wait" barely
survives describing.

Hawking's result (settled theory, never yet observed — the document tags it exactly
that way) says black holes evaporate. But a hole this size has a temperature around a
billionth of a billionth of a degree above absolute zero — *colder than the sky around
it*. So first it must wait for the whole universe to dilute until space itself is colder
than the hole. Only then does it begin, slowly, to bleed. The document calls it a **cold
source**: not an explosion but a leak, returning its entire mass to the world over
roughly **10^100 years** — a one followed by a hundred zeros.

The physics of evaporation is cruel at the end, though: the smaller the hole, the hotter
it burns, and the hotter it burns, the faster it shrinks. It is a firework that lights
its own fuse. The final instant of a hole that once weighed a hundred billion suns is a
speck of about **1.5 grams** — lighter than a coin — at a temperature around 10^25
kelvin. And then: a *flash*.

## Act 5 — One flash, one bubble, one aeon

Here is the framework's central bet, tagged as the hypothesis it is. The vacuum of our
universe may not be the ground floor — only a floor stable enough to stand on. Coleman
and De Luccia showed that such a vacuum can tunnel to a lower one by nucleating a
bubble; ordinarily the odds are absurdly small — *unless something seeds it*. Gregory,
Moss and Withers showed that **a tiny black hole is an excellent seed**.

So: the survivor's terminal flash, at the Planck-scale endpoint of its evaporation, is
the one struck match. It ignites one bubble. **One flash, one bubble, one aeon.**

Seen from inside, the bubble wall is not a wall rushing toward anyone — it is the
inside observers' *past*. On their own time-slicing, the new aeon starts hot
*everywhere at once*, smooth and uniform by the symmetry of the bubble itself, with no
fine-tuning ordered from outside. The enormous energy of the hot start is not borrowed
from anywhere: it is the old vacuum's latent energy converting in place. And dark
matter is not carried over from the previous chapter — it is minted fresh in the heat
of each new beginning.

## Act 6 — What about the second law?

Cyclic universes have historically all died of the same disease: entropy accumulates
every round, so after enough rounds nothing interesting should remain — yet our chapter
is interesting. The LMU answer: entropy is never *reset*, it is **diluted** — into a
base layer (L0) that the framework takes as an axiom to be unbounded. The new aeon's
arrow of time re-arms because a hot *smooth* start is a low-entropy start relative to
the freshly reopened ceiling: the first drop of ink on a new sheet of paper, not the
old sheet imperfectly erased.

## Closing — two open wounds, three appointments

The document states its own two unsolved problems on page one. **One:** does the fire
actually relight every round — is the new vacuum "seedable" again? That is a live,
field-wide dispute, not a private gap. **Two:** an unbounded L0 walks straight into the
measure problem of eternal-inflation cosmology, which nobody has solved.

And it keeps three appointments with reality: **DESI-Y5 (~2027)** — does dark energy
thaw; the **BHEX era (2030s)** — which way black-hole spin points; **LiteBIRD** — a
primordial gravitational-wave signature near r ≈ 0.009. Hit, and the wiring holds.
Miss, and the framework takes the damage in public, at thresholds it fixed in advance.

That is the whole story: a universe that ends as quietly as anything can end — and
hands the next one its first page with the final flash of the most patient thing that
ever existed.
