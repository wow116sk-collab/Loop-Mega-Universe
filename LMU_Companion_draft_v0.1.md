# (ii) The 1996 question

In 1996 the author was a merchant in Bangkok with no physics training and one
stubborn question: **when a black hole swallows something, where does it go?**
Not as a figure of speech — as bookkeeping. A star falls in; the books of the
outside universe close its account. Is the account *destroyed*, or
*transferred*? And if transferred — could a beginning somewhere be the far
side of an ending somewhere else? Could our own hot beginning be the inside
view of something that, from outside, looked like an ending?

That question has a respectable history the author did not know in 1996.
The picture of a universe living inside a black-hole horizon was published by
**Pathria in 1972**, and its modern realization — a bounce inside the horizon,
in Einstein–Cartan gravity — is **Popławski (2010)**. [Fact] The author's
first version (V1, ~1996) was independently built on the same instinct, and
the authoritative document now credits that ancestry explicitly at the exact
place in the framework where the instinct still lives (the collapsing-branch
bookkeeping of the V_min decision record). Rule R7 again: when the literature
owns your childhood idea, you write the owners' names on it.

What followed was thirty years of versions. Almost everything in V1 is dead —
killed by observations the author later learned to check against (the Bullet
Cluster alone executed an entire family of dark-matter ideas; section vii) —
and the question itself slowly changed shape. It stopped being "is the
universe inside a black hole?" and became something more modest and more
testable: **can the known equations of physics, wired together without
modification, close a loop** — matter climbing into black holes, black holes
evaporating, the cold thin end of one history being re-read as the hot dense
start of another — **and at exactly which welds does the wiring fail?** The

# (iii) The ladder, told

Begin with a fact you can check from a city rooftop on a clear night: the
universe sorts itself. Gas falls into stars, stars gather into galaxies,
galaxies hold central black holes, galaxies swarm into clusters, and at the
center of a great cluster sits one brightest galaxy whose black hole has
eaten better than all its neighbours. The framework's first move is a
**[Design]** choice about how to *rank* this hierarchy: not by mass, but by
**position in the eating process**. That ranking is the author's own, and it
runs:

- **L4** — the food: gas and stars, anything not yet behind a horizon;
- **L3** — a galaxy's central black hole: eats its own galaxy's supply;
- **L2** — a cluster-center (BCG) black hole: eats *other galaxies'* holes
  by mergers;
- **L1** — **the survivor**: the hole that wins every merger its island will
  ever offer, and is eventually the last gravitationally bound thing left.

Read the L1 definition again, because it is the framework's signature and the
author's own: **L1 is defined by survival, not by size.** [Design] A modest
hole in a poor neighbourhood can be an L1; a giant in a rich one may merely be
a meal. Mass is an outcome. Position in the eating order is the variable.

**From picture to machine.** The .tex turns this picture into equations of
motion — and here the honesty rules bite immediately, because *every one of
those equations belongs to somebody else*. Gas capture is Bondi (1952);
growth-by-light-limit is Salpeter (1964), with the dense-cocoon
super-Eddington channel from Inayoshi, Haiman & Ostriker (2016); the sinking
of companions is Chandrasekhar's dynamical friction (1943) in the
galaxy-assembly setting of White & Rees (1978), with the last gravitational-
wave inspiral from Peters (1964); spin-up to the limit is Thorne (1974), spin
*loss* through randomly-oriented mergers is Hughes & Blandford (2003) with
Berti & Volonteri (2008); the self-regulating ceiling — a hole grows until
its own wind shuts off its food — is Silk & Rees (1998) and King (2003),
normalized on the measured M–σ relation of McConnell & Ma (2013); ejection by
merger recoil rides González et al. (2007) and Campanelli et al. (2007) on
NFW (1996) escape speeds; starvation quenching is Bullock et al. (2000); and
the whole-population bookkeeping is Smoluchowski's coagulation equation
(1916) seeded through Press & Schechter (1974). [Fact] / [Fact-theory] The
author's contribution is the four state variables, the eating-position
ranking, and the assembly — the wiring. [Design]

**What the machine does.** It is a *forward map from environment to fate*.
Tell it what a young hole is born into — how deep its host's gravitational
well is, how much gas it is fed, how many companions it can eat — and it
returns where that hole ends up: final mass, final spin, rung on the ladder,
and whether it gets ejected or starved on the way. You do not tell it the
answer; you tell it the neighbourhood. Integrated forward in four different
environments, one set of equations returns the four observed kinds of object.
[Fact-theory] (the runs) / [Hypothesis] (the reading)

# (iv) The mountain

Dark energy, in this framework, is not a constant. It is a **field** — one
quantity, written A, filling each zone, with energy stored in its
displacement like a ball resting partway up a bowl. The bowl is the simplest
one physics offers, V = ½m²A² — a parabola whose bottom sits at exactly
zero. None of this machinery is the author's: the rolling-scalar idea is
Ratra & Peebles (1988) and Wetterich (1988), its dark-energy form is
Caldwell, Dave & Steinhardt (1998), and the "thawing" behaviour class is
Caldwell & Linder (2005) and Scherrer & Sen (2008). [Fact-theory] The
author's part is the *job description*: the field must (a) accelerate the
zone now — the only observed fact — and (b) weaken toward A → 0, so the zone
can go quiet and the loop can close. [Design] Remarkably, (a) and (b) are
the same motion seen from two ends: a ball rolling down does both.

**Three parties, one tug-of-war.** The field's equation of motion has three
terms you can feel in your hands: *inertia* (a rolling ball keeps rolling),
*Hubble friction* (expansion drags on the motion like honey), and the
*restoring force* (the slope of the bowl). Early on, friction wins: the ball
is frozen partway up the bowl, the field behaves exactly like a constant,
w = −1. As expansion slows the friction thins, the ball begins to roll —
the field "thaws" — and w lifts off −1. There is a wall here that no tuning
can move: a canonical field's pressure-to-density ratio can *never* go below
−1 (Vikman 2005); the integration confirms min w = −1.0000. [Fact-theory]
Hold that wall in mind; the falsifier in section (vi) stands on it.

# (v) The crossing

Now stand at the end of an island's history and look forward. The ladder is
finished; the survivor is alone. Its spin has radiated away (Page 1976), and
over 2.10×10¹⁰⁰ years the hole itself leaks into radiation, faster and
faster, ending in a runaway flash whose final output is **all massless** —
light and gravitational waves, nothing with rest mass. [Fact-theory]
(Hawking 1975; the time recomputed under R6) Everything massive that escaped
the hole over the aeons — ejected corpses, thinned debris — drifts in an
ever-flatter, ever-quieter space: the reservoir, L0, where H → 0 and, for
the first time since the bang, a total energy is even *definable* (flat
spacetime has the time-symmetry that conservation requires; an expanding one
does not). [Fact-theory]

**Penrose's move, in words.** Here is the idea the framework borrows whole
from Penrose (2010), with his name on it. Massless physics has no ruler. A
photon carries no clock and no meter stick; stretch all of spacetime by any
smoothly varying factor and the equations of light cannot tell. So take the
cold, thin, late end of a universe — temperature around 10⁻²⁰ K from the
last holes' final whispers — and *re-scale the ruler*: multiply the metric
by a factor Ω² chosen so that what was infinitely late and infinitely dilute
reads, in the new units, as infinitesimally early and infinitely dense. The
cold end and a hot beginning are, to massless physics, **the same geometry
in different units.** The "blueshift" implied — about 10⁴⁷ in temperature —
is not derivable from inside; the .tex proves it cannot be (the entropy that
would set it is conformally invariant, so there is no ratio to compute) and
files it as an *irreducible*, set-beyond-the-horizon number, in the same
honest box as the other boundary conditions. [Open], permanently and by
proof, not by laziness.

## A. Numbers and scales

**log₁₀ x** — counts *digits*, not size. log₁₀ = 122 means "a 1 with 122 zeros."
The whole document breathes in log-space; differences of "a few" here are factors
of thousands.

**dex** — one step of ×10. The relic offset "+0.22 dex above M–σ" means the black
holes weigh ~1.7× more than the relation predicts — noticeable, not monstrous.

**e and e-folds (N)** — one e-fold multiplies the universe's size by e ≈ 2.718.
N = 100 e-folds is growth by ×10⁴³. "Budget of e-folds" = how many doublings-ish
the phase can afford.

**e⁻²ᴺ, e⁻³ᴺ, e⁻⁴ᴺ** — a leftover divided by the same factor at *every* step.
After N = 100 steps the e⁻²ᴺ channel keeps one part in 10⁸⁷. *Where the picture
matters:* exponential decay beats any polynomial growth — this single fact is the
entire crossover-cleanliness argument.

**σ (significance)** — how many error-bar widths the measurement sits from the
boring answer. 2σ = interesting, 3σ = serious hint, 5σ = discovery. *Caution:*
σ values are never rounded up; 2.69σ is 2.69σ.

**ρ(w₀, wₐ) = −0.894 (correlation)** — when the fit pushes w₀ up, wₐ must come
down. The uncertainty region is a tilted cigar, not a circle; distances to it must
be measured along the tilt — which is why the "joint locus distance" is the honest
number.

**Leave-one-out (LOO)** — pull one card out of the tower and see if the result
stands. When dropping UGC 2698 flips r = −0.72 to +0.24, the "correlation" *was*
that one card. [The R2 rule in statistical clothing.]

## B. The expansion bookkeeping

**a(t), the scale factor** — the universe's zoom level. a = 1 today; a = 2 means
every untethered distance has doubled.

**H = ȧ/a, the Hubble rate** — growth *per unit of itself*: "what fraction bigger
per second," not a speed. *Caution:* H falling does not mean expansion is ending;
H = constant is exponential growth in disguise.

**ä/a, the acceleration** — whether the zoom is speeding up or slowing down. The
A-field's signature is a *mountain*: ä/a climbs, peaks at a = 0.99 (≈ today), and
descends — versus ΛCDM's permanent plateau.

**Friedmann equation** — the budget sheet: (growth rate)² = sum of everything in
the box, each ingredient diluting at its own rate as the box grows. [Friedmann 1922]

**w = p/ρ, the equation of state** — a substance's character when space stretches.
Matter: w = 0, thins as the volume. Radiation: w = ⅓, thins faster (each photon
also redshifts). w = −1: does not thin at all — every new cubic meter arrives
pre-filled, paid by the work of negative pressure.

**Comoving vs. proper distance** — a grid painted on the rubber sheet vs. a tape
measure laid across it. Galaxies sit still on the painted grid while the grid
itself stretches.

**Horizon** — the farthest light can have come from (past horizon) or will ever
reach (event horizon). **Super-horizon mode** = a wave longer than the whole
visible patch: the diver inside the tsunami — too long to see, only an
all-ocean instrument (DESI as altimeter) can read it.

**Conformal time η** — a clock that ticks ever slower as the universe grows, tuned
so light always travels at 45° on the chart. It is the bookkeeping that lets
"before the bang" and "after the bang" sit on one page.

## C. The A-field machinery

**V(A), the potential** — the hill the field rolls on. V = ½m²A² is a parabolic
valley whose floor sits at exactly zero — and "exactly zero" is the entire item-(B)
wall.

**Klein–Gordon with 3HȦ friction** — a ball rolling downhill while expansion acts
as friction. **Thawing**: while H > m the friction pins the ball (frozen, acts like
a cosmological constant); when H drops below m the ball wakes and rolls — w lifts
off −1.

**Oscillation → ⟨w⟩ = 0** — once underdamped, the ball swings through the valley
floor; averaged over swings it behaves exactly like matter and dilutes as a⁻³
[Turner 1983]. The dark energy literally turns to dust.

**Shooting method** — adjust the launch (A_i or m) until the trajectory lands on
today's measured Ω_DE = 0.69. Two *different* launch strategies landing on the same
(w₀, wₐ) point is the cross-validation, not a coincidence.

**Radau / DOP853** — careful step-by-step walkers for differential equations.
"Integrated" in this document means *walked numerically, every step checked* — not
solved by a formula and trusted.

**CPL: w(a) = w₀ + wₐ(1−a)** — a straight-line stand-in for a curve, honest only
near today. *Caution:* extrapolated past a = 1 it predicts w > +1, which no
physical field here ever does; the stand-in fails, not the field.

## D. Calculus in one breath

**∫ (integral)** — Leibniz's elongated S for *summa*: slice the thing into
uncountably many slivers, add them back. The limit is *exact*, not an estimate.
*Where the picture breaks:* "adding small pieces" misleads the moment the integrand
goes negative — pieces cancel, totals can vanish.

**d/dt, dx** — "a little bit of": the instantaneous rate, the direction of the
next tiny step.

**Differential equation** — a purely local rule: *given where you are, here is
your next step.* Walk the rule forward and it draws the entire history. The
document's spine (KG + Friedmann) is two such rules holding hands.

## E. Geometry at the crossing

**Ω²g (conformal rescale)** — swap the ruler everywhere at once, keeping every
angle and every light-cone. Shapes survive, sizes don't. This is the Penrose move
that makes an infinite future and a next bang meet on one surface.

**C^k smoothness / "exactly C^{2,1}"** — how clean the weld is: value, slope, and
curvature all match across the junction (C²); the *next* derivative jolts (C³
log-periodically blocked). C² is precisely what Einstein's equations require —
the weld passes with zero margin.

**Weyl tensor (E and B parts)** — the part of gravity *not* pinned to local
matter: tides and gravitational waves, the part that travels. "Weyl cleanliness"
= the weld carries no inherited tidal scar above the e⁻²ᴺ leftover.

**Killing vector** — a direction in which you can slide the geometry and nothing
changes; each one purchases a conservation law [Noether 1918]. Expanding space has
no time-slide — which is why total energy is *legally* non-conserved there.

**Frobenius indices** — the short menu of behaviors a field is even *allowed* to
have at t = 0. The stiff t^(−1/2) branch has no slot on the menu, so its amplitude
must vanish on the surface — that is condition G2-c, said in words.

**Nariai mass** — the largest black hole that fits inside a given cosmic horizon;
as H → 0 the ceiling rises without bound. Coefficient c³/(3√3·GH), not the naive
c³/(2GH).

## F. Statistics of the sky

**P(k), A_s, n_s** — the recipe of the initial lumps: how much lumpiness at each
size. A_s is the overall amplitude (~10⁻⁵ contrast), n_s the tilt (slightly more
power at large sizes, 0.9649).

**f_NL** — whether the lumps are pure coin-flip Gaussian or carry a correlated
signature. Measured consistent with zero (−0.9 ± 5.1) — a hard target any
imported generator must hit.

**Jeans length λ_J** — the tipping size between pressure and gravity: smaller
clouds bounce, larger clouds collapse. The item-(F) gate is the statement that
island-scale modes sit far above λ_J once the field oscillates.
